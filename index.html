🤝 Going without a buyer's agent?

You can contact the listing agent directly. They represent the seller, but they can still handle the transaction. Many buyers also hire a real estate attorney — costs vary by state — often $500–$1,500 — to review contracts and handle the closing. That's usually far less than a full buyer's agent commission.

✓ Fully supported on GOZ

📋 What about the NAR settlement?

Since August 2024, you must sign a buyer representation agreement before touring most homes — and that locks in a commission rate. On GOZ, you see what the seller already agreed to offer before you commit to anything. Know your exposure before you fall in love with the house.

✓ Commission shown pre-tour, always

💡 Can the seller's commission cover me?

If the seller is offering a buyer's agent commission (shown on every GOZ listing), and you bring a buyer's agent, that fee covers your agent. If you go without an agent, that commission may stay with the seller — or you can negotiate it as a price reduction. Either way, you'll know the number before you walk through the door.

✓ No surprises at closing
