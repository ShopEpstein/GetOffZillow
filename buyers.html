These aren't complaints. They're documented, sourced, and costing buyers real money. Here's what you should know before you start your search.

🙈

Commissions Hidden Until You're Already In

Zillow, Redfin, and Realtor.com don't show you what the seller is offering the buyer's agent. You find out after you've toured, after you've fallen in love, after you've signed a buyer rep agreement locking you in. GOZ shows it before any of that.

🏦

Your Agent Might Be Working for Their Lender

A federal class-action (Hagens Berman, 2024) alleges Zillow's Premier Agent program steers buyers toward Zillow Home Loans — creating a financial incentive for agents to push a specific lender over a better deal for you. GOZ has no mortgage arm. No referral bonuses. No conflict of interest.

Hagens Berman class-action, 2024
⚖️

Federal Scrutiny Is Growing

The FTC sued Zillow and Redfin in 2025 for allegedly coordinating to suppress competition in rental advertising. RICO claims are being explored in separate litigation. The largest real estate platforms are under more legal pressure than at any point in their history.

FTC complaint, 2025
💸

Lead Auctions Mean You Get Whoever Paid Most

Zillow's Premier Agent lets agents pay to appear in your ZIP code. When you click "contact agent," your info goes to whoever won that auction — not necessarily the most experienced, available, or appropriate agent for your situation. GOZ shows agents directly. No auction. No middleman.

Zillow Premier Agent program
🌊

Flood and Climate Risk Was Hidden From You

Zillow removed wildfire and flood risk data from listings in 2024 after sellers complained it hurt home values. They chose seller revenue over your ability to make an informed decision about the biggest purchase of your life. We don't remove inconvenient data.

Grist, 2024

You Are the Product

Your search history, your home preferences, your financial signals, and your contact info are monetized — sold to advertisers, lenders, and data brokers. Our only revenue is a technology marketing fee when a deal closes. Your data is not ours to sell.

Why the NAR Settlement Changed Everything for Buyers

Before August 2024, you could tour homes and then decide whether to use a buyer's agent. Now you must sign a buyer representation agreement before touring — locking in what you'll pay your agent upfront. If the seller is only offering 2% and you agreed to 3%, you may owe the 1% difference at closing. On a $400,000 home, that's $4,000 out of your pocket — and most platforms still don't show you what each seller is offering before you commit. GOZ does. That's not a feature. That's the whole point.
